Travel Consultant Reference

Beyond the Booking

Customer profiling guide for spectator sports travel

🏆

Taking a booking is easy. Knowing who you're booking it for, and what matters to them, that's what brings them back every season.

Order-Taker vs Travel Consultant
❌  Order-Taker
  • Quotes flights and accommodation without opening the CRM profile first
  • Treats every enquiry like it's this customer's first time travelling with us
  • Ignores past trips, past niggles, and preferences already on file
  • Asks the same generic questions no matter who's calling
  • Misses the small things that turn a booking into a relationship
  • Forgets to log anything new for the next person who picks up the file
→
✅  Travel Consultant
  • Pulls up the FreshCRM profile before saying a word
  • Knows what this traveller has booked before, and how it went
  • References past trips naturally, without needing to be reminded
  • Asks questions that show they've actually been paying attention
  • Spots what would make this specific trip easier or more memorable
  • Updates the profile so the next conversation starts smarter than this one
What This Sounds Like In Practice
💬

"I see you stayed at the Royal Palm last time, what stood out for you about that stay? Would you like to do something similar again, or try somewhere different this time round?"

This is what using FreshCRM well sounds like: specific, references what's already on file, and builds on it instead of starting from zero or re-asking for preferences the customer has already given.

The Six Profile Pillars
🗂️ 1. Check FreshCRM First
Before you say anything
  • What has this traveller booked with us before, and when?
  • Are there dietary requirements, hotel preferences, or special requests already logged from a previous trip?
  • What's their trip count or loyalty status to date?
Why it matters
  • The CRM is already telling you most of what you need to know
  • Re-asking what's already on file reads as not paying attention
🎯 The richest data point in this conversation exists before the call starts
📋 2. Past Travel History
Check the record first
  • Which events or destinations have they travelled to with us before?
  • What went well last time? What caused friction?
  • Is this a repeat trip they take every year, or something new for them?
Ask if there's a gap in the file
  • "Have you been to an event like this before?"
  • "What's worked well for you in the past, and what hasn't?"
  • "Anything from last time you'd want done differently?"
📂 Past experience, good or bad, is the strongest signal of what they need this time
👥 3. Who You're Actually Dealing With
Establish this early
  • How many are travelling, and what's the relationship between them, family, friends, a regular travel group?
  • Who's making the decision, and who's simply along for the trip?
  • Is this a group that's travelled together before, or a new combination of people?
Why it matters
  • Knowing who's actually deciding saves both of you time
  • A returning group has expectations set by their last trip together
👤 The person on the phone isn't always the person paying or deciding
⭐ 4. Where Edusport Adds Value
This is where the relationship is built
  • The event gets them there, accommodation, transport, and the extras are where Edusport's value (and margin) actually sits
  • Comfort, proximity to the venue, transport on the day, an accommodation upgrade, what's worth offering this traveller?
  • What would make this particular trip easier, early check-in, a private transfer, a tip about the venue?
Use what's already on file
  • "Last time an early checkout worked well for you, want to do the same again?"
  • Only ask outright if there's a genuine gap, never re-ask what's already logged in FreshCRM
✨ Specific beats generic, and it shows you were paying attention
🏅 5. Loyalty & Relationship Signals
Listen for these patterns
  • Do they travel to the same event every year, or follow the same team?
  • Have they referred friends or family to Edusport?
  • Do they come back to you as soon as a fixture list or schedule is announced?
Deepen the relationship
  • "Is there anything else on the calendar this year we should keep an eye on for you?"
  • "Would it help to plan your trips for the season together, rather than one at a time?"
🔗 Loyal customers deserve a proactive call, not just a quick reply
🌟 6. The Emotional Stakes
This kind of travel is personal
  • Is this a first-time trip to a major event, a final, a long-awaited milestone?
  • Is the group celebrating something, an anniversary, a retirement, a reunion?
  • Have they been trying to get to this event for years?
Acknowledge the moment
  • "What's the story behind this trip?"
  • Even a small gesture (a note, a personalised itinerary) shows this isn't just a transaction
🌟 The best trips become memories, help make them that way
Know What Kind of Trip This Is — It Changes Everything
📅  Different trip types, different priorities
🏟️
Local / Regional Fixture

Focus: simple logistics, short turnaround, possible day-trip option

🧳
Touring / Away Trip

Focus: multi-day accommodation away from home, transport on fixture day

🌍
International Tournament

Focus: visas, passports, insurance, multi-week stays, time zones

🎟️
Multi-Day Festival Event

Focus: ticket packages across several days, hospitality options, flexible itinerary

⚠️  Red Flags — Qualify These Before Going Further
No confirmed decision-maker or budget authority on a group booking
International trip with no mention of travel insurance
Tight timeline with no flexibility acknowledged by the customer
A returning customer being treated like a first-time enquiry
Asking for preferences, dietary needs, or trip details that are already logged in FreshCRM
Large group with no single point of contact identified
Why This Matters
📅
Repeat Events
Sporting calendars repeat every year. A traveller who trusts you with one trip calls you first for the next.
🤝
Referral Chains
Fans talk to other fans. One well-handled trip gets you introduced to their travel group, their friends, their next booking.
🛡️
Fewer Problems
Checking the profile and history upfront means knowing where a trip can go wrong before it does.
📋

After every interaction: Log the trip, any new preferences, issues, and what they mention about future plans into FreshCRM. The next consultant who picks up this file should know everything you do.
