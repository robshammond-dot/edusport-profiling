Beyond the Order

Customer profiling guide for sports travel coordinators

🏅

Taking a booking is easy. Building a customer relationship that returns every season — that takes knowing more than just the destination and the numbers.

Order-Taker vs Sports Travel Coordinator
❌  Order-Taker
  • Hears "we need flights and a hotel for 20 people" and starts quoting
  • Doesn't ask which sport, event, or team it's for
  • Ignores past trips or past problems
  • Never asks about the mix of experience in the group
  • Treats it as a logistical transaction and nothing more
  • Misses the moments that create loyalty
→
✅  Coordinator
  • Understands the team, the event, and the stakes before quoting
  • Asks what went well and what was difficult last time
  • Knows who the decision-maker is vs who the participants are
  • Anticipates logistical pain points before they become problems
  • Treats the trip as a moment that matters to real people
  • Becomes the person they call again next season
The Six Profile Pillars
🏆 1. Sport & Event Context
Always establish first
  • Which sport and which specific event or tournament?
  • Are they competing, coaching, or spectating?
  • What's the significance of this particular event — league, nationals, international?
Why it matters
  • Competing teams need early check-in, meal plans, gear storage
  • Spectator groups need flexible timing and access to the venue
🎯 The event type shapes every decision downstream
👥 2. Group Makeup
Understand who's in the group
  • How many players, how many support staff, how many parents/spectators?
  • Age group — juniors, seniors, mixed?
  • Any minors? If yes, supervision and safeguarding must be confirmed.
  • Is there a team manager or designated lead contact?
The coordinator isn't always the decision-maker
  • Confirm who has final sign-off on the booking
  • Ask who your operational contact will be on the ground
🧒 Junior groups have very different requirements to senior squads
📋 3. Past Trip History
Check the record first
  • Have they travelled with Edusport before? Pull the file.
  • What events have they attended in previous years?
  • Any complaints, delays, or operational issues last time?
Ask if new
  • "Have you organised a trip like this before?"
  • "What worked well last time? What caused headaches?"
  • "What's the one thing you'd want to make sure goes better this time?"
📂 Past issues are the biggest loyalty opportunity — fix them proactively
🗺️ 4. Logistical Complexity
Scope the complexity early
  • Single flight or multiple legs? Domestic or international?
  • Equipment to transport — kit bags, bikes, sports gear, medical supplies?
  • Multiple departure points or one group point of departure?
  • Accommodation near the venue, or transfers required?
Pre-empt the problems
  • "Do any team members have dietary requirements or medical needs?"
  • "Will you need flexibility on return dates depending on results?"
⚙️ Complex logistics need lead time — qualify this early
🏅 5. Loyalty & Relationship Signals
Listen for these patterns
  • Has the club or school been booking the same event annually?
  • Do they refer other teams or organisations to you?
  • Do they come back within hours of the season draw with a new enquiry?
Deepen the relationship
  • "Are there other tournaments this season we should be aware of?"
  • "Would it help to do an annual calendar review together so we can plan ahead?"
  • "Are there other coaches at your club who'd benefit from this kind of support?"
🔗 Loyal accounts deserve proactive reach-outs, not just reactive responses
❤️ 6. The Emotional Stakes
Sports travel is personal
  • Is this a first national trip for a junior team?
  • Is it a farewell tour for a matric group or retiring coach?
  • Has the team qualified for something significant after years of trying?
Acknowledge the moment
  • "This sounds like a big one — what's the story behind this trip?"
  • Even small gestures (a congratulations note, a personalised itinerary) reinforce that this isn't just a transaction
🌟 The best sports trips become memories — help make them that way
Know the Event Context — It Changes Everything
📅  Different event types, different priorities
🏟️
National Tournament

Focus: proximity to venue, early arrival, match-day flexibility, group discipline

✈️
International Tour

Focus: visas, passports, insurance, kit allowances, time zone management

🎓
Schools Sport

Focus: safeguarding for minors, teacher supervision ratios, parent communication

👁️
Spectator Group

Focus: match tickets, flexible check-out, dining options, match schedule awareness

⚠️  Red Flags — Qualify These Before Going Further
Group with minors and no confirmed supervising adult ratio
Departure dates tied to results with no contingency plan discussed
International trip with no mention of travel insurance
Large group with no confirmed decision-maker or budget authority
Equipment-heavy group (bikes, boards, gear) with no excess baggage discussion
Very tight timeline with no flexibility acknowledged by the customer
Why This Matters
📅
Annual Accounts
Sports seasons repeat. A club that trusts you with nationals this year will call you first next year, and the year after.
🤝
Referral Chains
Coaches and sports managers talk. One well-managed trip gets you introduced to other coaches, other clubs, other sports codes.
🛡️
Fewer Problems
A proper profile upfront prevents the 11 pm calls. Knowing the group means knowing where it can go wrong before it does.
📋

After every interaction: Log the sport, event type, group size, key contacts, past issues, and any upcoming events in the CRM. Sports groups travel in cycles — your notes from this trip become your head start on the next one.
